    \textbf{Abstract} \\[0.3cm]

\end{center}

\normalsize

Knowledge-graph-aware (KG-aware) recommenders work well on dense KGs, but sparse KGs are underexplored. On a sparse review-derived KG ($2.4$ aspect edges per item), four representative methods (KGAT, KGCL, MCCLK, KGRec) are all outperformed by pure LightGCN: each embeds the KG into the scoring pipeline with no structural switch to disengage it when unreliable, letting KG noise contaminate the collaborative gradient.

We propose RA-GARK (Rationale-Aware Gating over Review-Aspect Knowledge graphs), which treats the KG as a gated side channel. A local-biased fusion gate, bias-initialized to $+5$, starts training as pure LightGCN and opens the KG channel only when gradients show it helps---structural graceful degradation. Each item's KG semantics are encoded as $A=4$ latent aspect slots, initialized from a truncated SVD of an IDF-weighted item--aspect co-occurrence matrix; softmax attention then selects the most salient slot. Under a fixed KG, RA-GARK reaches NDCG@20 $= 0.1238$, beating KGRec by $+13.1\%$ and pure LightGCN by $+5.0\%$. An ablation study attributes $7.0\%$ NDCG@$20$ to the softmax-vs-sigmoid choice and $5.3\%$ each to the biased gate and the KG-SVD init. The attention-normalization observation is a single-dataset hypothesis awaiting replication.\\[0.5\baselineskip]
\textbf{Keywords: knowledge-graph-aware recommendation; graceful degradation; fusion gate; sparse knowledge graph; collaborative filtering.}
